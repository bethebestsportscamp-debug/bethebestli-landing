Conversion Architecture

CTA & Funnel Strategy

The Parent Decision Journey

Key Insight

The buyer is the parent. The user is the athlete. Every CTA must answer the parent's real question: "Will this help my kid get to the next level — and can I trust these people?"

Awareness — "Who is BTB?"

Traffic sources: Google search, Instagram, word-of-mouth referrals, tournament exposure.

Goal: Get them to the site. Get them to stay 60+ seconds.

Watch Highlights View Programs Read Blog Post Follow on Instagram
Consideration — "Is BTB right for us?"

Behavior: Browsing program pages, reading coach bios, checking schedule, comparing with other clubs.

Goal: Capture email. Build trust. Answer objections.

Download Tryout Guide (PDF) View Coaching Staff See College Commits Read Parent Testimonials Subscribe to Newsletter
Conversion — "We're in."

Behavior: Ready to commit. Looking for registration link, tryout dates, payment info.

Goal: Zero friction to register. One-click where possible.

Register for Tryouts Reserve Your Spot Schedule a Call Pay Deposit
Retention — "We're staying."

Behavior: Current families. Need schedule, portal access, community.

Goal: Keep families engaged. Reduce churn. Drive referrals.

Parent Portal Login BTB Academy Access Refer a Friend View Schedule

CTA Button Hierarchy

LevelLabelStylePlacement
Primary"Register for Tryouts"Red button, white text, arrow iconHero, nav bar, sticky mobile bar, every page footer
Secondary"Find Your Program"White button, red text or black outlineHero, program pages

Sticky Mobile CTA

On mobile, a persistent bottom bar with "Register for Tryouts →" stays visible at all times. This alone can increase mobile conversion by 15–25%. Disappears only on the registration page itself.

Exit Intent Popup (Desktop Only)

Triggered when cursor leaves viewport. Offer: "Free Tryout Prep Guide" — email capture in exchange for a PDF covering what to bring, how to stand out, and the BTB evaluation criteria. This converts 3–5% of abandoning visitors.

Social Proof & Credibility

Trust-Building System

The Trust Stack

Parents don't sign up because of features. They sign up because they trust you with their kid. Every page needs at least 3 trust signals visible without scrolling.

Tier 1 — Hard Credentials (Non-Negotiable)

These must be on every page, either in the header, hero, or immediately below.

  • College Commit Count: "85+ College Placements" with logo bar. This is the single most powerful trust signal in youth lacrosse. Update in real-time as commits happen.
  • Coaching Staff Credentials: Where coaches played (D1/D2/D3), years of experience, certifications. Every coach page needs a headshot, bio, and "Why I Coach" quote.
  • SafeSport & Background Checks: Badge in footer + dedicated /safety/ page. Parents Google this. Be proactive.
  • US Lacrosse Member Club: Official badge. Signals legitimacy to parents who don't know the landscape.
  • Years Operating: "Est. [Year]" in the logo or footer. Longevity = legitimacy.

Tier 2 — Social Proof (Emotional Trust)

  • Parent Video Testimonials: 60-second clips. Format: "My son was [before]. After BTB, [after]." Name and face visible. Minimum 3 on homepage.
  • Player Success Stories: Full-page features on athletes who committed. Journey narrative: "From BTB Futures to D1 Scholarship." Shareable format.
  • Google Reviews Widget: Embed your Google Business reviews (target: 4.8+ stars, 50+ reviews). Parents check Google reviews before they check your website.
  • "By the Numbers" Dashboard: 500+ athletes, 85+ commits, 24 teams, 6 national titles, 50 coaches. Large font, high-contrast, visible within 3 seconds of landing.
  • Instagram Feed: Embedded. Shows daily life, wins, culture. Proves the program is active and thriving right now.

Tier 3 — Authority Signals

  • Media Mentions: "As Seen In" bar with logos: Inside Lacrosse, Newsday, LaxPower, local news. Even one feature is enough.
  • Equipment / Brand Partnerships: If you have a deal with STX, Maverik, Epoch, etc. — logo bar with "Official Equipment Partner."
  • Facility Photos: High-quality photos of where you practice and play. Turf fields, indoor facilities, branded gear. Visual quality = perceived program quality.
  • College Coach Quotes: "BTB players show up prepared." — Attributed to actual college coaches who recruit your players. Massive credibility.

Color Ratio Rule

70% black/dark, 20% white, 10% red. Red is the accent — not the background. This creates the Nike EYBL-level premium feel. The moment you put red backgrounds on large sections, it starts looking like a pizza shop flyer. Red is for buttons, headlines, borders, and badges only.

Typography

Display / HeadlinesBebas Neue400 (regular)H1–H3, stats, nav logo, buttons, badges. Always uppercase.
Body / UIMontserrat300–700Paragraphs, nav links, form inputs, captions, descriptions.

Photography Direction

  • Action shots only. No posed group photos with arms crossed. Every image should show athletes in motion — shooting, dodging, celebrating, competing.
  • Dark, high-contrast editing. Desaturate slightly, boost contrast, add subtle vignette. Think ESPN highlight reel, not summer camp brochure.
  • Consistent crop ratios. Hero: 16:9. Cards: 4:3. Headshots: 1:1. No exceptions.
  • No stock photos. Every image should be from actual BTB practices, games, or events. Authenticity beats polish.
  • Red accent overlays. Use red gradient overlays on dark photos for hero sections and CTA backgrounds.
