ABOUT BTB

BTB (Be The Best) Lacrosse is Long Island's premier lacrosse development program. We build complete players through structured training, film study, small-group coaching, and a culture of accountability.

BTB stands apart with filmed tryouts for transparency, varsity-level coaching staff, a structured development model that combines on-field training with film study, and a culture-first approach that builds character alongside skill.

BTB serves players from 3rd grade through high school across our travel teams, Futures program, academy, and camps.

BTB Lacrosse is based on Long Island, New York. Practices and training sessions rotate between several Long Island facilities to provide the best playing surfaces for our athletes.

✎

TRYOUTS & REGISTRATION

Tryouts are typically held in the fall for the upcoming travel season. Exact dates are announced on our website and social media channels. Register your interest to be notified when tryout dates are set.

BTB uses filmed tryouts so every evaluation is transparent and objective. Players are assessed on stick skills, lacrosse IQ, athleticism, coachability, and effort. Film is reviewed by our coaching staff to ensure fair placement on competitive rosters.

Program fees vary by level and season. Visit our registration page or contact us directly for current pricing. We are committed to providing exceptional value for the level of coaching and development offered.

Yes. BTB films every tryout session. This ensures complete transparency in the evaluation process and gives families confidence that roster decisions are based on merit, not politics. It's one of the things that sets BTB apart.

🏆

TRAVEL TEAMS

BTB fields 24+ teams across multiple age groups and divisions for both boys and girls, competing at local, regional, and national tournaments.

Our travel teams compete in top-tier regional and national tournaments throughout the year, providing exposure to college coaches and high-level competition. Tournament schedules are shared with families at the start of each season.

Practice schedules vary by team and season. Travel teams typically practice 1-2 times per week with tournaments on weekends during the competitive season. Schedules are designed to balance development with family time.

BTB travel teams compete at the highest levels, with 85+ college commitments and counting. Our teams regularly place in top tournaments, and our development pipeline prepares athletes for varsity and collegiate lacrosse.

🏠

FUTURES PROGRAM

The Futures Program is BTB's introductory development track for younger players (typically 3rd-5th grade). It focuses on fundamental skill building, game understanding, and preparing athletes for travel-level competition in a supportive environment.

The Futures Program is designed for players in 3rd through 5th grade who are developing their skills and preparing for travel-level play. It's the ideal starting point for young athletes new to competitive lacrosse.

Futures serves as a direct pipeline to our travel teams. Players who develop through the Futures program are well-prepared for travel tryouts and often transition seamlessly into our competitive rosters.

⛺

CAMPS & CLINICS

BTB runs camps primarily during the summer months, with additional clinics and skills sessions during school breaks. Check our registration page for upcoming dates and availability.

Our camps are coached by the same varsity-level staff that runs our travel and academy programs, ensuring consistent, high-quality instruction.

Yes! BTB camps are open to all players regardless of current team affiliation. Camps are a great way to experience BTB coaching and training methods firsthand.

🥍

EQUIPMENT & LOGISTICS

Players need a lacrosse stick, helmet (boys), goggles (girls), gloves, cleats, and a mouthguard. Specific gear requirements vary by program and are communicated upon registration.

Practices are held at various field locations across Long Island. Specific locations are communicated to registered families at the start of each season.

Weather cancellations are communicated via email and team messaging as early as possible. Cancelled sessions are rescheduled when possible, or alternative indoor training may be arranged.

Yes, travel team players receive BTB uniforms as part of their program fees. Additional BTB gear and apparel is available for purchase throughout the season.
