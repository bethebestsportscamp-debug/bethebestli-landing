Our Standard

This Program Is Not For Everyone

BTB is for families who understand that development is earned through discipline, repetition, and accountability. We do not recruit talent. We build players who are prepared for the next level.

01
Culture First

Every player earns their place through effort, attitude, and consistency. There are no shortcuts here. Coachability is not optional — it is the foundation of everything we do.

02
Accountability

Players are accountable to their coaches, their teammates, and themselves. We review film. We track progress. We expect players to own their development, not wait for it to happen.

03
Consistency Over Hype

We do not promise exposure. We promise a structured environment where players train with intention, compete with purpose, and improve measurably over time.

04
Earned Success

Playing time, positions, and opportunities are earned through preparation and performance. The players who put in the work see the results. That is the only formula we believe in.

05
Long-Term Development

We build four-year athletes, not one-season highlights. Our development pathway is designed to take a player from foundational skills through varsity readiness and into college preparation.

06
Real Coaching

Our coaches prepare practice plans, study film, and bring structured curricula to every session. We do not run open scrimmages and call it training. Every minute on the field has a purpose.

Development Model

How BTB Actually Develops Players

A structured, repeatable system designed to build complete lacrosse players across every phase of the game.

Small-Group Training

Maximum 8:1 player-to-coach ratio during skill sessions. Every player gets individual attention, coaching cues, and real-time correction. No standing in lines. No wasted reps.

Film Study Integration

Game and practice film is reviewed weekly. Players learn to see the field, recognize defensive schemes, and make better decisions under pressure. Film is where lacrosse IQ is built.

Structured Practice Plans

Every practice follows a written plan with clear objectives, timed segments, and progressive skill-building. Our curriculum is documented, reviewed, and improved every season.

Progressive Skill Phases

Development is organized into four phases across a 16-week cycle: Foundation, Connection, Expansion, and Execution. Players build skills in sequence, not at random.

Intentional Repetition

We track reps, measure progress, and assign specific skill work between sessions. Development is not accidental. It is planned, measured, and held accountable.

Complete Player Focus

Stick skills, shooting, defense, offense, transition, and game IQ are all developed systematically. We build complete players, not one-dimensional athletes.

Film Study

The Game Is Won Before the Whistle

Film study is the single most underused development tool in youth lacrosse. At BTB, it is required. Players who understand the game on film perform at a higher level on the field.

01

Game IQ Development

Players learn to read defensive formations, recognize slide packages, identify open players, and make faster decisions. This is not guesswork — it is taught through structured film review.

02

Decision-Making Under Pressure

Film slows the game down. Players see what happened, why it happened, and what the better decision was. Over time, this translates to faster, more confident play in real time.

03

Accountability Through Review

Every player sees their own performance. There is no hiding on film. Missed assignments, poor positioning, and lazy habits are identified and corrected. This is how real accountability works.

04

Position-Specific Learning

Attackmen study feeding angles and shot selection. Middies study transition reads. Defensemen study approach technique and slide timing. Goalies study shot tendencies. Every position has its own film curriculum.

05

Self-Scouting

Players learn to scout themselves — identifying their own tendencies, strengths, and weaknesses. This is the skill that separates college-ready players from everyone else.

06

Opponent Preparation

Before key matchups, teams review opponent film. Players learn what to expect and come into games with a plan, not just effort. Preparation replaces guesswork.

Coaches Hub

Real Coaches. Real Standards.

Every coach in this program is held to the same standard as every player. Preparation, accountability, and intentional leadership are not optional.

Coaching Philosophy

Our coaches teach through structure, repetition, and progression. We do not yell from the sideline and hope players figure it out. Every correction comes with a coaching point and a path to improvement.

Practice Planning

Coaches submit written practice plans before every session. Each plan follows the seasonal curriculum, includes timed drill segments, and targets specific skills for that phase of development.

Leadership & Accountability

Coaches are evaluated on player development outcomes, not win-loss records. They lead by example, hold players accountable, and create an environment where serious development happens.

Continuing Education

All BTB coaches complete US Lacrosse certification, SafeSport training, and internal coaching development. We invest in our coaching staff because the quality of coaching determines the quality of the program.

50+ Drill Playbook

Our coaches operate from a shared drillbook with 50+ documented drills across 8 categories. Every drill includes purpose, setup, execution steps, coaching points, and video references.

Background & Safety

Every coach passes a background check and maintains current SafeSport certification. Player safety is non-negotiable. Parents should never have to wonder about the adults leading their children.
